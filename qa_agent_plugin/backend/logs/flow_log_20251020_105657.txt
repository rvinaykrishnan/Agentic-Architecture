Query: "Explain artificial intelligence"

Answer:
Artificial intelligence (AI) is essentially the broad concept of creating machines that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence. Think of it as teaching computers to 'think' and 'learn'. This doesn't mean robots becoming self-aware and taking over the world (at least not yet!), but rather focusing on specific tasks. 

There are two main categories of AI:

*   **Narrow or Weak AI:** This type of AI is designed and trained for a specific task. Examples include spam filters, recommendation systems (like Netflix suggesting what to watch next), and even self-driving cars (though these are becoming more sophisticated).  These systems excel at their defined task but can't generalize to other areas. Most AI systems we use today fall into this category.

*   **General or Strong AI:** This is the hypothetical type of AI that possesses human-level intelligence.  A strong AI could understand, learn, adapt, and implement knowledge in a wide range of contexts, just like a human. We haven't achieved strong AI yet, and it remains a significant research goal.

Technically, AI achieves its goals through a combination of techniques. Some key ones include:

*   **Machine Learning (ML):**  This involves training algorithms to learn from data without being explicitly programmed.  For instance, you can train a machine learning model to recognize cats in images by showing it many pictures of cats and letting it figure out the patterns.
*   **Deep Learning (DL):** A subset of machine learning that uses artificial neural networks with multiple layers (hence "deep") to analyze data and learn complex patterns.
