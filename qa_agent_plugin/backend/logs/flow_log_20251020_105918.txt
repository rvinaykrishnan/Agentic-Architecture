Query: "Explain artificial intelligence"

Answer:
Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a multifaceted field concerned with designing and developing intelligent agents, systems capable of perceiving their environment and taking actions to maximize their chances of achieving defined goals. This broad definition encompasses a wide range of approaches, each with its strengths and weaknesses. At its core, AI aims to replicate, simulate, or extend human-like intelligence in machines.

**Key Paradigms and Approaches:**

*   **Symbolic AI (Good Old-Fashioned AI - GOFAI):** This approach, prominent in the early days of AI, focuses on representing knowledge using symbolic structures (e.g., logic, rules, semantic networks). Reasoning is performed by manipulating these symbols according to predefined rules. Expert systems and early natural language processing (NLP) systems are examples of symbolic AI. While effective for well-defined domains with structured knowledge, it struggles with handling uncertainty, ambiguity, and large amounts of unstructured data. Frame Problem is a major issue: how can an agent maintain a consistent representation of the world as actions are performed and the world changes.

*   **Subsymbolic AI (Connectionism):** This approach, exemplified by neural networks, emphasizes learning from data. Instead of explicitly programming rules, connectionist systems learn patterns and relationships from examples. This is achieved by adjusting the connections (weights) between interconnected nodes (neurons).
